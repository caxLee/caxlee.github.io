Faces of MIT: Ylana Lopez

Ylana Lopez is the assistant director of academics and events at the Martin Trust Center for MIT Entrepreneurship. She leads various programs and events at the Trust Center and was part of a startup that went through the flagship accelerator program, delta v. Lopez transitioned from a full-time job to focus on the startup and later joined the Trust Center as an academic coordinator before being promoted to her current role. She is passionate about education and entrepreneurship, and her work involves supporting various programs, including MIT Fuse and t=0. Lopez believes in the power of entrepreneurship to change the world and is part of a collaborative team at the Trust Center. Trust Center managing director Bill Aulet praises Lopez for her understanding of students and her creative program development. Lopez describes the MIT community as inspiring and welcoming, and advises newcomers to break out of their bubbles and connect with people across different departments for potential collaborations.
